# Remote Work Syndicate — SEO & Content Roadmap v1.0

Recommended file path:

`/docs/07-seo.md`

This document defines the SEO and content acquisition strategy for Remote Work Syndicate.

Remote Work Syndicate is a content-led acquisition business with product-led retention.

The goal is not to compete immediately for generic keywords like “remote jobs.” The goal is to win high-intent long-tail searches around AI work platforms, platform comparisons, interview guides, application guidance, salary/pay expectations, country-specific AI remote work, and role-specific remote AI jobs.

---

## 1. SEO Strategy Summary

Remote Work Syndicate should become the most useful independent content hub for people searching:

- Which AI work platform should I use?
- Is Mercor legit?
- Mercor vs micro1
- How to pass Outlier assessment
- Best AI trainer jobs in India
- DataAnnotation vs Outlier
- Remote AI jobs that pay in USD
- AI evaluator jobs for beginners
- Best platforms for coding AI trainers
- How long does Mercor take to respond?

The SEO strategy is based on five content pillars:

1. Platform pages
2. Comparison pages
3. Role/category pages
4. Guides and interview prep pages
5. Country-specific pages

All roads should lead users toward:

- Platform Match Quiz
- Relevant platform pages
- Relevant jobs
- Newsletter signup
- Outcome contribution CTA
- Apply/referral links

---

## 2. SEO Positioning

Remote Work Syndicate is not a generic job board.

SEO positioning:

> The independent intelligence layer for AI remote work platforms.

The site should rank for decision-oriented searches, not just job-listing searches.

### We should avoid over-prioritizing

- remote jobs
- work from home jobs
- online jobs
- freelance jobs
- software engineer jobs

These are too broad and competitive early.

### We should prioritize

- platform-specific searches
- platform comparison searches
- AI trainer / AI evaluator searches
- application and interview searches
- country-specific AI remote work searches
- pay and legitimacy searches

## 13. Programmatic SEO

Programmatic SEO should be used carefully.

Good programmatic pages:

- Platform pages
- Comparison pages
- Category pages
- Country pages

Bad programmatic pages:

- Thin pages with no unique insight
- Hundreds of fake city/job combinations
- Auto-generated pages without verified data
- Duplicate comparison pages with swapped names only

### Rule

A page should not be indexed unless it has unique value.

---

## 14. Indexing Rules

### Index

- Homepage
- Active platform pages
- Active job pages
- Published guides
- Published comparisons
- Category pages with enough jobs/content
- Country pages with enough content

### Noindex

- Internal search results
- Thin tag pages
- Expired jobs, unless they have long-term SEO value and are clearly marked expired
- Quiz results pages, if personalized
- Unreviewed community report pages
- Admin pages

---

## 15. Expired Jobs SEO Policy

Expired jobs should not look active.

Options:

### Option A

Mark expired and noindex.

Use when job has no long-term value.

### Option B

Keep indexed as archived.

Use when page has traffic and can redirect users to similar active roles.

Required archived copy:

```text
This role appears to be expired or unavailable. Explore similar verified AI remote work opportunities below.
```

Recommended:

For V1, mark expired jobs as expired and noindex unless they attract traffic.

## 26. Codex SEO Implementation Instructions

Codex should:

1. Build SEO-friendly route structure from the architecture doc.
2. Use metadata functions for every page type.
3. Add sitemap and robots routes.
4. Add breadcrumb components.
5. Add JSON-LD components.
6. Make content pages statically renderable where possible.
7. Ensure expired jobs do not appear active.
8. Include last verified / last updated dates.
9. Add internal linking blocks.
10. Avoid generating fake statistics.
11. Use placeholder content only when clearly labeled.
12. Keep noindex logic for thin/personalized pages.

1. Homepage is indexable.
